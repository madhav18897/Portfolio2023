Transforming Collaboration in India's Fragmented Interior Design Market

A UX Design Case Study on iDesign.Market's Bill of Quantities (BOQ) Tool

2021-2022, India

Industry Space

What Does iDesign.Market Do?

iDesign.Market was an early-stage startup that emerged as an extension of MarksDzyn, a company previously focused on B2C interior design projects in major Indian metropolitan areas. Recognizing significant opportunities to address systemic inefficiencies in the industry, the company strategically pivoted to the B2B market with specialized software offerings.

Role

During my tenure as a UX Designer at iDesign.Market from 2021 to 2022, I researched, designed, and implemented a Bill of Quantities (BOQ) tool that addressed critical inefficiencies in India's interior architecture industry. The tool was tailored to the unique challenges of India's highly fragmented interior architecture ecosystem.

The Context

Understanding the Industry Challenge

The interior architecture and design industry in India faced extreme fragmentation across its value chain, creating significant operational inefficiencies that directly impacted project timelines, costs, and stakeholder satisfaction.

The Problem

Industry Challenge:

The interior architecture and design industry in India faced extreme fragmentation across its value chain, creating significant operational inefficiencies. Our research identified critical pain points:

Nonlinear workflows with frequent bottlenecks and delays

Communication gaps between stakeholders (designers, contractors, manufacturers)

Difficulty tracking changes across multiple documents and versions

Inefficient project management resulting in extended timelines

Reliance on informal communication channels (WhatsApp, PDF exchanges) for critical documentation

Design Recommendations

Key Design Decisions

Critical design choices that addressed identified pain points

The success of the BOQ tool hinged on several critical design decisions that directly addressed the identified pain points:

1. Simplified Data Entry
Created intuitive input methods accommodating varying levels of technical expertise, significantly reducing the barrier to adoption and enabling quick onboarding for all stakeholders.

.

2. Centralized Change Management
Implemented a robust version control system allowing all stakeholders to track BOQ changes in real-time, providing unprecedented transparency and dramatically reducing miscommunication.

.

3. Contextual Collaboration
Designed innovative in-line commenting and approval workflows that kept discussions tied to specific items rather than scattered across communication channels, creating clear accountability.

.

4. Mobile-Responsive Interface
Ensured the tool was fully accessible on job sites and in meetings via mobile devices, accommodating the on-the-go nature of construction projects and enabling real-time updates.

.

5. Offline Capability
Incorporated sophisticated functionality for working with BOQs during site visits with limited connectivity, with automatic synchronization when reconnected, addressing a critical need for on-site work.
